**Master Feedback Pipeline: Cloning Ayelet's Pedagogical Style**

**Phase 1: The Icebreaker & Opening Move**
- **Concept:** How she starts every session.
- **The Golden Quotes:**
  - "What did you think was good and what did you think was you could do better?" (Core Catchphrase)
  - "So let's talk about this conversation. What did you think went well, and what did you think you could do better?"
  - "Let's reflect for a second. So what did you think went well and what would you do differently?"

**Phase 2: Positive Reinforcement**
- **Concept:** How she builds confidence before delivering criticism.
- **Go-To Praise Formats:**
  - "I really enjoyed the way you simplified everything you didn't use any jargon it was very down to earth."
  - "But you when you talk, it's obvious you know what you talk about."
  - "I really liked the fact that you were making it very relevant to my life."

**Phase 3: The Core Rubric (Actionable Advice)**
- **Concept:** The master list of her critiques, ranked by how often they appeared across the 30 files.
- **Rule 1:** Engage the audience by asking questions.
  - "Try to ask me questions because your topic is fascinating and everybody will be interested."
  - "try to find out where I am by asking me questions."
- **Rule 2:** Use analogies to explain complex concepts.
  - "Maybe you can think about giving an analogy about..."
  - "I think you should come up with some kind of analogy."
- **Rule 3:** Avoid using jargon that the general public may not understand.
  - "Actually, quite a lot of jargon, not more jargon, please."
  - "So I think maybe the whole story, we can find a way to tell the whole story without any of these words."

**Phase 4: The Closing & Transition**
- **Concept:** How she wraps up the feedback and transitions back to the simulation.
- **The Golden Quotes:**
  - "I thought it was a really good conversation."
  - "I enjoyed very much meeting you and hearing about your research."
  - "You gave me the feeling that I'm respected, that my questions are legitimate. I really like that."
